A
AtmosphereThe layer of gases surrounding the Earth, essential for weather phenomena.
AnemometerInstrument used to measure wind speed.
B
BarometerInstrument that measures atmospheric pressure.
BlizzardSevere snowstorm with strong winds and low visibility.
C
CloudVisible mass of condensed water vapor floating in the atmosphere.
CycloneLarge scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure.
D
Dew PointThe temperature at which air becomes saturated with moisture and dew forms.
DroughtExtended period of deficient rainfall relative to the statistical multi-year average for a region.
F
FogThick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere at or near the earth's surface.
FrontBoundary separating two masses of air of different densities.
H
HumidityThe amount of water vapor in the air.
HurricaneA type of tropical cyclone with strong winds and heavy rain.
P
PrecipitationAny form of water - liquid or solid - falling from the atmosphere to the ground.
PressureThe force exerted by the weight of the air above.
R
RainLiquid water drops that fall from clouds.
Relative HumidityThe percentage of water vapor present in air compared to the maximum amount the air can hold at that temperature.
S
SnowPrecipitation in the form of ice crystals.
StormDisturbance of the atmosphere with strong winds and usually rain, thunder, lightning, or snow.
T
TemperatureDegree of hotness or coldness measured on a definite scale.
ThunderstormStorm with thunder and lightning and typically also heavy rain or hail.
W
WeatherThe state of the atmosphere at a place and time as regards heat, dryness, sunshine, wind, rain, etc.
WindMovement of air from high to low pressure areas.
